# Clan — Positioning & Go-To-Market

## The One-Liner

**Clan is family care coordination that works the way families actually work — no accounts, no cloud, no complexity.**

## The Problem

53 million family caregivers in the US juggle medications, appointments, and daily updates across spreadsheets, group texts, and sticky notes. The existing apps (Caring Village, Medisafe, CaringBridge) all require:
- Account signup (friction for elderly users)
- Cloud storage (trust issue for medical data)
- Monthly subscriptions ($15-25/mo adds up)
- Complex UIs (confusing for 70+ users)

## The Gap

**Nobody occupies this intersection:**

- Family care coordination (meds + appointments + tasks + updates)
- 100% local-first (data stays on devices)
- No accounts required for core features
- Designed for seniors (large text, simple navigation)
- Free core (pay only for optional sync)

## Target Users

### Primary: Sandwich Generation (35-55)
Caring for aging parents + own kids. Tech-literate but overwhelmed. Values privacy, hates subscriptions.

### Secondary: Long-Distance Caregiver
Lives in another state. Needs shared view of appointments, meds, daily updates. Frustrated with phone tag.

### Tertiary: Senior Care Recipient (65+)
Needs simple daily view. Large text, one-button check-in. May use a dedicated iPad or tablet.
